1. DR Tracker Onboarding
    1. The Basics
      1. This is a free to use application to prepare for and execute Disaster Recovery Exercises. This application is approved for WK use by all DR teams in 2027 assuming all proceeds as planned. One of my goals with this page and the DR Tracker application is to make it easier to get up to speed on how you to prepare for and complete Disaster Recovery Exercises with your team. Having that said, not all information presented here has been thoroughly reviewed by the various DR teams such as Architects, DR Governance, etc. If you have any questions about what is required to succesfully complete your DR Exercises, please contact your Architect, your App Owner, Elena Kalmykova and/or DR Governance. What I share here is my understanding not official WK guidance. If you are using the DR Tracker Application, and you need help, please contact gbs-es-disaster-recovery@wolterskluwer.com.
    2. Onboarding Spreadsheet
      1. There is an on onboarding spreadsheet (download link below) which needs to be completed and sent to gbs-es-disaster-recovery@wolterskluwer.com in order to initiate the onboarding process. You can complete the spreadsheet in stages. For example, you can provide your application names and user setup information so you can use these related screens before completing the Hour By Hour Plan templates. In order to make it possible to have everything ready for your DR Exercise, you need to send the fully completed spreadsheet to gbs-es-disaster-recovery, at least 6 weeks before your test date. There is only 1 person supporting this process, and it is in his additional time, not part of regular work hours. There will likely be clarifying questions for the HxH Plan and getting the plan into the database requires a fair of data preparation. For more information, please download the spreadsheet.

  2. Business Process
    1. My 1-2-3 steps on DR are:
      1. Complete the DR Design
      2. Update the DR Documentation
      3. Standup the DR Infrastructure
      4. Complete the DR Prep Tasks To Ensure That Everything Will Work on Your Test Days (e.g. Failover, Failback, Data Corruption)
      5. Conduct the Tabletop Exercise (A Tabletop Excersise is a Test Practice Run)
      6. Setup the Go-No Go Meeting
      7. Conduct High Availability, Failover, Failback, Data Corruption, Evidence of Volumetric Attack Defense. Get Evidence for Key Tasks and Upload to WKDR Governance Sharepoint
      8. Submit Post Exercise Report to Your DR Coordinator (Assigned by WKDR Governance)

  3. Highlight DR Tracker Application Features
    1. Feature List
      1. Navigation: You navigate through the application by making selections in the drop downs in the upper right hand corner of the page. Year and Page are the key drop downs, for lower level users. For higher level users, Platform is also an important drop down. You can navigate through by changing the page.
      2. Hour By Hour Plan: The hxh plan is really the star of the show. The green button on the left hand side drives the start and stop of a task, set’s the actual start date when started and end date when completed, and changes the status (Not Started->In Progress->Completed) along the way. The little i to the right of Row ID displays additional task information when you click on it. When it’s red, it means that there are task details or jira/RITM info present. The planned start and end date are automatically calculated each time the page is loaded. The start date/time of the test, duration of the task and the predecessor(s) are the inputs into figuring out the start and end date.
      3. Broadcast Feature: There is a change broadcast feature. So when two browsers are open, and a record is added, changed or deleted, it will broadcast to the other browser. So if you and I are logged in and viewing the same page, and you make a change, it will broadcast to my browser and a notification is shown in my browser window (like an alert).
      4. Drag & Drop: In the main table section, at the very left hand side of the table, you can drag rows up and down in the sequence if you have the required security level. Your changes will be saved.
      5. Filter Rows By Text Search Field: The Search Text box above the main table section, will filter the rows that are visible in the table based on finding matches across all of the columns.
      6. Filter Rows By Column Filter: At the top of most columns in the main table section, there is the ability for file the rows in the table by choosing an option from the drop down or entering search text depending on the column data.
      7. Independently Setting Up An Account: If you have a wk e-mail address, just start the login process, and the application will walk you through next steps via e-mail. Upon initial setup, you we will recieve an e-mail with a few questions, and be asked to forward the e-mail to gbs-es-disaster-recovery@wolterskluwer.com.
